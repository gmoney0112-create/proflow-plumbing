Emergency Service
Do you offer 24/7 emergency plumbing service?
Yes. ProFlow Plumbing answers every call 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. Plumbing emergencies don't keep business hours, and neither do we.
How quickly can you respond to an emergency?
Our target is to have a licensed plumber at your door within 60 minutes for emergency calls in the San Antonio area. Response time may vary slightly based on location and current dispatch, but we always prioritize emergencies.
What counts as a plumbing emergency?
Burst or actively leaking pipes, sewage backups, no water supply to the home, gas line issues, flooding, and water heater failures causing safety concerns are all emergencies. When in doubt, call us — we'll help you assess the situation at no charge.
What should I do while waiting for a plumber in an emergency?
Shut off the water supply immediately. Your main shutoff valve is typically near the water meter or where the supply line enters the home. Turn off electricity to any flooded areas. Do not use drains if you suspect a sewage backup. Then call us at (210) 555-0100 and we'll walk you through next steps while we're en route.

Common Plumbing Questions
What causes low water pressure in my home?
Common causes include a partially closed main shutoff valve, mineral buildup inside pipes or fixtures, a failing pressure regulator, or a leak somewhere in the system. Older galvanized steel pipes can also corrode and restrict flow over time. We can diagnose the root cause quickly and give you a clear path to a fix.
Should I repair or replace my water heater?
If your water heater is under 8 years old and the problem is isolated (a bad element, thermostat, or anode rod), repair usually makes financial sense. If it's over 10–12 years old, leaking from the tank, or showing signs of rust in the water, replacement is almost always the better long-term investment. We'll give you an honest assessment of both options with pricing for each.
Why does my drain keep clogging?
Recurring clogs typically mean there's a deeper problem: grease buildup deeper in the line, root intrusion, a damaged or misaligned pipe, or an undersized drain. A camera inspection is the fastest way to see exactly what's happening and prevent the cycle from repeating.
